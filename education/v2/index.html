About Us

FXStreet reacts to the Online Trading Academy crisis

In light of the recent events involving the OTA being sued and in trial for misleading practices to their students, FXStreet has opened a new live webinar service to provide users with a similar training experience to that the Online Trading Academy, aiming to guarantee that no OTA user gets affected by a possible shutdown of the online academy.

FXStreet considers that regardless of the situation, users are always the affected party, being the sudden disappearance of an unregulated broker, or as it seems it is the case now, the prompt closedown of the OTA.

“In our 20 years in the market we have always cared about the trader, and we have always vouched for education and to keep on empowering our users. Trading is a risky activity and the more you learn about trading the better decisions you may take, and it is still never a guarantee of success.” Setxi Fernández, CEO at FXStreet.com stated. “ Traders are always misled to believe that trading helps everyone can become rich easily, and unfortunately this is not the case. Trading requires practice and knowledge of the markets, and no one can guarantee any success rate”

Following their firm belief in the importance of education and their compromise to the traders, FXStreet has decided to launch a new webinar service to offer all OTA users a chance to continue with their education on fxstreet.com.

17 weekly live-webinars have been created to help users continue with their training. The topics covered in said webinars are: Forex (4 sessions a week), Stocks (4 sessions a week), Futures (4 sessions a week), Options (2 sessions a week), Long Term strategies (2 sessions a week) and a special weekly session on questions and answers.

The service does not only offer webinars, but a dedicated monitoring and many years of experience on the market to back it up. So far, experts like Jose Blasco have already joined this FXStreet initiative, with more to join soon. To round this up and to show their compromise with the traders this service will be free of charge for three months to those users registered in the OTA, with no obligation to continue once the free period is over. “Prices in the market for Trading courses and webinars are way too expensive, abusive to a certain extent.” Mr Fernádez said.
